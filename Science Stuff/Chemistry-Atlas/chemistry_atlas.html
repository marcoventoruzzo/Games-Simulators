04 · BALANCING EQUATIONS

Count atoms. Never invent them.

“In a chemical reaction, matter is neither created nor destroyed.” — Lavoisier’s law of conservation of mass

Why practise this?

Balanced coefficients are chemical bookkeeping. They let laboratories predict reactant quantities, product yields, waste, energy and cost—from a school flask to an industrial reactor.

05 · LANGUAGE & MEASURE

A mole is a chemist’s counting unit.

Just as “a dozen” means 12 objects, one mole means exactly 6.02214076 × 10²³ specified entities. It bridges invisible particles and measurable laboratory mass.

6.02214076 × 10²³entities per mole · Avogadro constant, exact

Formula mass is the sum of each atom’s atomic mass. Multiplying moles by Avogadro’s constant gives the number of molecules or formula units.

Seven small experiments. Seven big ideas.

Animated previews connect an observation to the particles behind it. Procedures are deliberately concise: carry them out only with a teacher, suitable PPE and your school’s approved protocol.

⚠ Safety is part of the science.

Wear goggles, tie back hair, label reagents, keep ignition sources controlled, and dispose of products as instructed. Never taste laboratory substances.
