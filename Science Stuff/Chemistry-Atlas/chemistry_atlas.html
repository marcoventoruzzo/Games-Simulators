Why two rows are shown below: La and Ac remain in group 3 after Ba and Ra. To keep the table compact, the fourteen elements that follow each—Ce–Lu (lanthanides) and Th–Lr (actinides)—are displayed as two detached rows. In atomic-number order they belong precisely between La and Hf, and between Ac and Rf; they mainly fill the 4f and 5f subshells.

04 · BALANCING EQUATIONS

Count atoms. Never invent them.

“In a chemical reaction, matter is neither created nor destroyed.” — Lavoisier’s law of conservation of mass

Why practise this?

Balanced coefficients are chemical bookkeeping. They let laboratories predict reactant quantities, product yields, waste, energy and cost—from a school flask to an industrial reactor.

05 · LANGUAGE & MEASURE

A mole is a chemist’s counting unit.

Just as “a dozen” means 12 objects, one mole means exactly 6.02214076 × 10²³ specified entities. It bridges invisible particles and measurable laboratory mass.

6.02214076 × 10²³entities per mole · Avogadro constant, exact

Formula mass is the sum of each atom’s atomic mass. Multiplying moles by Avogadro’s constant gives the number of molecules or formula units.

06 · PRACTICAL CHEMISTRY

Six small experiments. Six big ideas.

Each static apparatus diagram is paired with a recipe-style outline, the expected observation and the chemical interpretation. Use established, risk-assessed procedures, suitable PPE and responsible disposal.

⚠ Safety is part of the science.

Use the safest available household-scale materials where indicated. Wear eye protection, label every substance, ventilate appropriately, control ignition sources and never seal a gas-producing reaction.
